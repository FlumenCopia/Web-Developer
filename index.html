03 Attendance & Working Hours

Office Hours

Standard working hours are 9:30 AM to 6:30 PM, Monday to Saturday. Core mandatory hours are 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Lunch break is 1:00 PM to 2:00 PM.

Late-Coming Policy

A grace period of 10 minutes is allowed. Late arrival is permitted a maximum of 3 times per calendar month. From the 4th late instance onward, a half-day salary deduction will apply per occurrence. Habitual late-coming (more than 5 times in a month) will be addressed through the performance warning system.

Contingency — Remote Work

FlumenX does not have a general Work From Home policy. All employees are expected to be present at the office during working hours. Remote work is permitted only in the following specific contingency situations, and requires explicit manager approval each time:

  • Power Failure: If office power is unavailable for more than 2 continuous hours and work cannot continue on battery, the manager may authorise working remotely for the remainder of that day only.
  • Hartal / Bandh: If a state or national strike prevents safe travel to office, employees may work from home that day. The CEO or Operations Manager will communicate this decision by 8:30 AM via the official WhatsApp group.
  • Natural Calamity: Floods, cyclones, or red-alert weather events. Safety takes priority — employees should inform their manager and work remotely if conditions are dangerous. No leave will be deducted in genuine calamity situations.

In all contingency cases: you must be reachable during office hours, attend all scheduled calls, and deliver daily targets as normal. Contingency remote work days do not accumulate or transfer to other days.

Daily Stand-Up

Mandatory team stand-up meeting every Monday at 10:00 AM. All team members must attend and actively participate. Silent attendance is noted and will be flagged after the second consecutive occurrence.

07 Disciplinary Framework

How warnings work: This section explains what happens when you violate policies. The intent is not punitive — it is to create a clear, fair, and documented process. All warnings are placed on record and affect performance ratings and increment eligibility.

Progressive Discipline Tiers

1
Verbal Warning
Issued for minor, first-time violations (minor lateness, small quality issues, missing a stand-up once). Documented internally by the manager. No formal record added to your HR file at this stage.
2
Written Warning (Level 1)
Issued for repeated minor violations or a first-time moderate violation. Formal email from HR. Added to your HR file. Affects quarterly bonus eligibility. You must sign acknowledgement.
3
Written Warning (Level 2) + Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
Issued for a third violation or escalation of moderate violations. Formal PIP is initiated — a 30-day structured improvement program. No increment or bonus during PIP. Continued failure leads to Tier 4.
4
Termination or Suspension
Issued when PIP fails, or immediately for serious offenses (data breach, client harassment, financial misconduct, gross insubordination, AWOL for 3+ days). Termination without notice period for gross misconduct. Final settlement cleared within 15 days.

Zero Tolerance Offenses (immediate termination): Sexual harassment, discrimination, intentional data leak/NDA breach, theft, fraud, and physical violence. No progressive discipline applies for these.

Appeal Process

Any employee may appeal a disciplinary decision to the CEO in writing within 7 days of receiving a warning. The CEO's decision after a fair hearing is final. Employees are encouraged to raise genuine concerns — this process is not meant to suppress feedback.

08 Resignation & Exit
  • Notice Period: 30 days (staff); 60 days (senior/leadership roles). Written resignation must be submitted via email to HR and the CEO.
  • Handover: All pending tasks, files, credentials, and client documentation must be transferred before the last working day.
  • Exit Interview: Conducted by HR. Honest feedback is encouraged and will not affect your reference letter or relieving documents.
  • Final Settlement: Salary, earned leave encashment, and all dues cleared within 15 working days. Relieving letter and experience certificate will be issued.
  • Company Property: All devices, access credentials, files, and assets must be returned on or before the last working day.
09a Data Security & Confidentiality

Client Data Protection

  • Store all client credentials exclusively in the company password manager — never in email, WhatsApp, personal notes, or plain text files.
  • Use VPN when accessing client accounts remotely or from personal devices.
  • Log out of all client accounts immediately after use. Never leave sessions open.
  • Never share client passwords, brand assets, documents, or confidential materials with any party outside the team.
  • Screenshotting confidential client documents or financial records is strictly prohibited.
  • Never access client accounts from personal devices unless explicitly authorised by your manager and the CEO.

Company Data Protection

  • Financial data: Accessible only by the Accounts Manager, CFO, and CEO. Never discuss client pricing, margins, or salary information with colleagues or external parties.
  • Strategic plans and client pipeline: Strictly confidential — not for external discussion under any circumstances.
  • Design assets and proprietary templates: Company-owned resources. May not be shared with external parties or removed from company systems upon resignation.
  • Client list: A company asset. Using client details for personal prospecting or disclosing to a third party is a terminable offence under your employment agreement.

Personal Device Policy

If you use a personal device for work tasks, antivirus software is mandatory. All company files on personal devices must be encrypted or stored only in the company cloud system. Upon resignation or termination, delete all company data — files, emails, app credentials, and any copies — from personal devices. Failure to do so may constitute a breach of your confidentiality agreement and may be pursued legally.

09b Team Meeting Participation

Active participation in all team meetings is mandatory. Silent attendance is treated as non-participation and is tracked in your performance record.

What Is Expected

  • Share your updates, blockers, and ideas clearly in every stand-up and review meeting.
  • When asked for input — respond. Silence is treated as disengagement, not neutrality.
  • Come prepared: review your task list and project status before attending any meeting.
  • Camera on for all video calls during contingency remote days.
  • Arrive on time — meetings start as scheduled. Late arrival without prior notice will be logged.
  • Follow through on action items assigned to you in meetings within the agreed timeframe.

Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

A PIP is initiated when a rating of 1 or 2 is received, or when disciplinary escalation reaches the third written warning. Duration: 90 days (3 months). A PIP is a structured support mechanism — not a punishment. The company commits resources and management time to help you succeed during this period.

  • Clear, specific, measurable improvement goals are defined at the outset.
  • Weekly check-ins with your manager are mandatory throughout the PIP period.
  • Training, mentoring, or additional support will be provided as needed.
  • Possible outcomes: Goals met — PIP closed, rating restored; Partial improvement — PIP extended by 30–60 days; No improvement — employment termination.

Salary & Compensation Details

  • Salary payment date: 5th to 12th of every calendar month via bank transfer. If the date falls on a public or bank holiday, payment is processed on the next working day.
  • Salary structure: CTC = Basic + HRA + Special Allowance + applicable bonuses. Full details are in your individual employment contract.
  • Annual increment: 5–15% based on performance rating. Reviewed annually, effective from the review month.
  • Training budget: ₹5,000 per employee per year, subject to manager approval. Can be used for online courses, professional certifications, industry workshops, or learning resources directly relevant to your role.
  • Performance bonus: Discretionary, based on individual performance and company revenue performance. Typically reviewed quarterly or annually.

09 Role Responsibilities — By Level

Junior Web Developer

  • Build assigned components, pages, or modules under Senior/Mid supervision.
  • Daily code commits — no uncommitted local work at end of day.
  • Write clean, commented, documented code. No "magic numbers" or unexplained logic.
  • Raise blockers within 2 hours — do not sit stuck in silence.
  • Complete code review feedback within 24 hours of receiving it.

Mid-Level Web Developer

  • Own assigned client projects end-to-end with Senior oversight on architecture decisions.
  • Write and maintain technical documentation for all projects.
  • Conduct code reviews for Junior developers.
  • Ensure all delivered work meets browser compatibility, mobile responsiveness, and performance standards (page load < 3 seconds, Lighthouse score >80).
  • Participate in client technical calls when required.

Senior Web Developer

  • Architect and lead complex web projects — Next.js, React, custom CMS, FLOXA, FXLayer.
  • Own all technical quality — code reviews, deployment pipelines, security audits.
  • Mentor Junior and Mid developers through code reviews and pair programming sessions.
  • Interface with CEO on product architecture decisions for FLOXA and Flumen OS.
  • Maintain all production environments — uptime SLA: >99.5%.
  • Resolve all production bugs within 24 hours (critical: within 4 hours).

11 Technical Standards & Security Policy

Code Standards

  • All repositories use Git. Branching strategy: main (production), develop, feature branches.
  • No direct pushes to main without PR review by Senior Developer.
  • Commit messages must be descriptive: feat: add WhatsApp login to FLOXA auth module
  • All code must pass linting and testing before merge.

Security Policy

Zero tolerance for: Hardcoding API keys, passwords, or secrets in code. All environment variables go in .env files — never committed to repositories. Client credentials must be stored in the approved secrets manager only. Any security vulnerability discovered must be reported to the Senior Dev and CEO within 1 hour.

Client Site Maintenance

  • All client sites on retainer must have weekly backup verification.
  • SSL certificates must be monitored — never allow expiry.
  • Any emergency maintenance must be communicated to the client and Operations Manager before execution.
